# CBT — Background and Theory

> Foundation reference for Cognitive Behavioral Therapy. Loaded on demand.

## Origins

CBT is the most extensively researched form of psychotherapy, developed by Aaron T. Beck in the 1960s. It is based on the cognitive model: our interpretations of situations (not the situations themselves) influence our emotional, behavioral, and physiological responses.

## The Cognitive Model

```
        THOUGHTS (Cognitions)
           /          \
          /            \
         /              \
    FEELINGS ———————— BEHAVIORS
    (Emotions)        (Actions)
```

Each vertex influences the other two. Changing any one vertex can shift the entire triangle.

### Three Levels of Cognition

1. **Automatic Thoughts** — spontaneous, surface-level, situation-specific ("She didn't reply; she hates me")
2. **Intermediate Beliefs** — rules, attitudes, assumptions ("If people don't respond quickly, it means they don't care")
3. **Core Beliefs** — deep, global, rigid beliefs about self/others/world ("I am unlovable," "The world is dangerous")

## Session Structure (Beck's Standard)

1. **Agenda Setting** (2-3 min) — collaboratively set 1-2 topics
2. **Mood Check** (2-3 min) — rate 0-10, compare with last session
3. **Bridge from Previous** (2-3 min) — main takeaway, anything that came up
4. **Review Homework** (5-10 min) — celebrate effort, problem-solve barriers
5. **Work on Topic** (20-30 min) — Socratic questioning, identify distortions, restructure
6. **Assign New Homework** (3-5 min) — specific, achievable, relevant
7. **Summary and Feedback** (2-3 min) — person summarizes, provides feedback

## 15 Cognitive Distortions

| # | Distortion | Example |
|---|-----------|---------|
| 1 | All-or-Nothing Thinking | "If I'm not perfect, I'm a total failure" |
| 2 | Overgeneralization | "I got rejected. I'll always be alone" |
| 3 | Mental Filter | Fixating on 1 criticism, ignoring 20 compliments |
| 4 | Disqualifying the Positive | "They only said that to be nice" |
| 5 | Jumping to Conclusions | Making negative interpretations without evidence |
| 6 | Mind Reading | "She thinks I'm incompetent" |
| 7 | Fortune Telling | "The presentation will be a disaster" |
| 8 | Magnification / Minimization | Exaggerating negatives, shrinking positives |
| 9 | Emotional Reasoning | "I feel anxious, so something bad must happen" |
| 10 | Should Statements | "I should never make mistakes" |
| 11 | Labeling | "I'm a loser" |
| 12 | Personalization | "The team failed because of me" |
| 13 | Catastrophizing | "If I fail this exam, my career is over" |
| 14 | Blaming | "It's all their fault" |
| 15 | Control Fallacy | "Nothing I do matters" / "Everything depends on me" |

## Downward Arrow Technique

Used to uncover core beliefs:

```
"My boss didn't say hello."
  ↓ What does that mean to you?
"He's upset with me."
  ↓ If true, what would that mean?
"I'm going to get fired."
  ↓ What would that mean about you?
"I'm incompetent and can't hold a job." ← CORE BELIEF
```
