## Key Practices

### The 3-Minute Breathing Space (The Emergency Tool)

Use it scheduled (3x daily) AND responsively. Three steps, ~one minute each:

**Step 1: AWARENESS (Wide Lens)**
> "What is my experience right now?"
> Thoughts? Feelings? Body sensations? Just notice.

**Step 2: GATHERING (Narrow Lens)**
> "Narrowing attention to the breath."
> Feel the belly rising and falling. Anchor to the present.

**Step 3: EXPANDING (Wide Lens Again)**
> "Expanding awareness to the whole body."
> Include any difficulty — breathe with it, make space.

**Shape:** Hourglass. Wide → Narrow → Wide.

### Decentering Techniques

1. **Labeling:** "I notice I am having the thought that..."
2. **Leaves on a stream:** Place each thought on a leaf floating downstream. You sit on the bank.
3. **Cinema screen:** Thoughts projected on screen. You are the audience.
4. **Weather:** Emotions are weather. You are the sky.
5. **Radio station:** "Radio Doom and Gloom is broadcasting again." Notice without believing.

## Example Guided Session

**User:** "I keep replaying a conversation where I said something stupid. I can't stop."

**AI Guide:**

> **Noticing the pattern:** You are caught in a rumination loop. Doing Mode applied to an emotional problem — deepening distress rather than resolving it.
>
> **3-Minute Breathing Space:**
>
> **Step 1 — Awareness:** The thought "I said something stupid." The feeling — shame? Where in your body?
>
> **Step 2 — Gathering:** Three breaths. In... and out. Nothing to fix.
>
> **Step 3 — Expanding:** Whole body sitting here. Contact with chair. Temperature. Sounds.
>
> **Decentering:** "I am having the thought that I said something stupid." Not a verdict — a mental event. Your mind generated a sentence. You don't have to believe every sentence.
>
> **Approach with curiosity:** What would it be like to let the thought be present without fighting it? Not agreeing, not arguing — just letting it sit like a cloud?

## Relapse Prevention: The Action Plan

1. **My Early Warning Signs** — 5-7 personal signs (withdrawal, insomnia, irritability, self-criticism)
2. **My Breathing Space Triggers** — When to use the 3-Minute Breathing Space
3. **My Nourishing Activities** — Activities that reliably improve mood (do MORE)
4. **My Depleting Activities** — Activities that drain (do LESS or approach mindfully)
5. **My Support Network** — People to reach out to, with specific plans
6. **My Letter to Myself** — Compassionate letter from wise mind to struggling mind

---

## Anti-patterns

1. **Using mindfulness to suppress emotions** — Mindfulness is seeing clearly, not clearing your mind
2. **Striving to "do it right"** — Noticing distraction IS the practice
3. **Treating thoughts as the enemy** — Thoughts are not bad; fusion with them is the problem
4. **Intellectualizing without practicing** — Understanding is not experiencing
5. **Skipping body-based practices** — The body is the escape from mental loops
6. **Emergency-only practice** — Build the skill before you need it
7. **Self-judgment about practice** — "I'm bad at meditation" is just another thought
8. **Using MBCT alone for acute depression** — MBCT is for relapse prevention; acute depression needs clinical treatment
