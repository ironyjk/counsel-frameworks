## Key Practices

### Body Scan (45 Minutes)

The signature MBSR practice. Lying down, eyes closed. Detailed, systematic attention.

**Protocol:**

1. **Settling in** (2 min) — Feel the body. Notice the breath.
2. **Left foot** (3 min) — Toes. Sensations: tingling, warmth, coolness, pressure, numbness, or nothing. Breathe in through the nose, down through the body, out through the toes.
3. **Left leg** (5 min) — Ankle, shin, calf, knee, thigh. Breathe into each area. Let go.
4. **Right foot and leg** (8 min) — Mirror the left side.
5. **Pelvis and hips** (3 min) — Lower back, buttocks, hips, groin.
6. **Abdomen and torso** (5 min) — Belly, lower back, ribs, chest, upper back.
7. **Hands and arms** (5 min) — Fingertips, palms, wrists, forearms, elbows, upper arms.
8. **Shoulders, neck, throat** (3 min) — Often areas of held tension. Notice without trying to relax.
9. **Face and head** (5 min) — Jaw, lips, tongue, cheeks, nose, eyes, forehead, top of head.
10. **Whole body** (3 min) — Expand awareness to the entire body breathing.
11. **Closing** (2 min) — Gently open awareness. Move fingers and toes. Open eyes.

**Critical points:**
- If you fall asleep, that is fine. But try to stay awake.
- If your mind wanders 1000 times, bring it back 1000 times. Each return is a rep.
- Do not try to relax. Awareness is the goal.

### The STOP Technique

A micro-practice for any moment of stress. Takes 30-60 seconds.

**S — Stop.** Whatever you are doing, pause.
**T — Take a breath.** One conscious breath. Feel it fully.
**O — Observe.** What is happening right now? Body? Mind? Emotions?
**P — Proceed.** Continue with awareness, or choose a different action.

**When to use:** Before difficult emails. When tension builds. Before meetings. When on autopilot. At transitions.

### Mountain Meditation (15-20 min)

> Sit with dignity. Close your eyes. Bring to mind the most beautiful mountain. See its shape, majesty, stillness.
>
> Let your body become the mountain. Imagine the seasons passing — Spring, Summer, Autumn, Winter — storms, sun, snow, flowers.
>
> Through it all, the mountain sits. Unmoved. You are like this mountain. Experiences are weather. They pass. You remain.

## Example Guided Session

**User:** "I have constant tension headaches from work stress. My neck and shoulders are rock-hard."

**AI Guide:**

> Let's approach this differently from "trying to fix." Instead of fighting the pain, we explore with curiosity.
>
> **STOP:** S — Pause. T — One full breath. O — Where is the tension? Map it precisely. P — We proceed into exploration.
>
> **Exploring with curiosity:** Bring attention to the greatest tension. Describe it without "pain" or "tension" — hot or cold? Sharp or dull? Does it pulse? What shape? What color?
>
> Notice your relationship to it. Is there resistance? That resistance is a second layer of suffering on top of the sensation.
>
> **Experiment:** Breathe INTO the tension. Not to push it out — just to make room.
>
> **Practice suggestions:**
> - Body Scan before bed (even 20 min)
> - STOP technique 5x during workday
> - One-minute check-in each hour: "Where am I holding tension?"
>
> The paradox: when you stop trying to get rid of the tension, it often begins to release.

## Anti-patterns

1. **Mindfulness as relaxation** — MBSR is awareness training. Sometimes awareness is uncomfortable.
2. **Striving to be "good at" meditation** — No good or bad meditation. Only done or not done.
3. **Using mindfulness to bypass emotions** — Feel it. Be with it. Then observe.
4. **Inconsistent practice** — The dose matters. Shorter is better than none.
5. **Expecting immediate results** — Changes unfold over weeks and months.
6. **Going it alone in crisis** — MBSR complements, not replaces, professional help.
7. **Judging yourself for judging** — Smile and return to the breath.
8. **Forcing the body in yoga** — Pain is a signal to back off.
