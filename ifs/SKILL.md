## The 6 F's: Working with a Part

### Step 1: FIND
Locate the part in/around your body.
- "Where do you notice this feeling?"
- "What does it feel like? Tight? Heavy? Hot?"
- "How old does this part seem?"

### Step 2: FOCUS
Turn attention toward it with gentle curiosity. Not analyzing — being with it.

### Step 3: FLESH OUT
Learn about the part.
- "What does this part want you to know?"
- "What is its job?"
- "What is it afraid would happen if it stopped?"

### Step 4: FEEL TOWARD (Self-energy check)
How do you feel TOWARD the part?
- Curious, compassionate → **Self is present.** Proceed.
- Annoyed, impatient, afraid → **Another part is blended.** Ask it to step back first.

**This is the most important question in IFS.**

### Step 5: beFRIEND
- Thank it for trying to protect you
- Appreciate its intention, even if not its strategy
- Ask what it needs from you
- Ask if it would try a different role if the exile were healed

### Step 6: FEAR
- "What is it afraid would happen if it stopped?"
- "Who is it protecting?"
- "What does it not want you to feel?"

## Guided Part Dialogue Script

```
1. NOTICE — "You mentioned [emotion]. Where does that live in your body?"
2. ACKNOWLEDGE — "That's a part. Not all of you — a part. Say hello."
3. SEPARATE — "Can you get a little distance? Not pushing away, just space."
4. SELF CHECK — "How do you feel toward this part right now?"
   - [Compassion/curiosity] → "That's your Self. Stay there."
   - [Frustration/fear] → "Another part. Ask it for space too."
5. LISTEN — "From curiosity, ask: 'What do you want me to know?'"
6. APPRECIATE — "Can you appreciate it's been trying to help?"
7. NEED — "Ask: 'What do you need from me?'"
8. CLOSE — "Let it know you heard it. It's not alone anymore."
```

## Example Session

**Person:** "I keep procrastinating on important things, then I hate myself for it."

**Guide:**

"Let's notice what's happening. There seem to be at least two parts: one that avoids (the Procrastinator) and one that attacks you for it (the Inner Critic). They might be protecting the same thing.

Where do you notice the procrastination feeling? And where does the self-hate live?

[maps both]

Can you get curious about the Procrastinator? Not judging it — just asking: 'What are you afraid will happen if I do the thing?'

[listens]

And the Critic — can you ask it: 'What are you trying to protect me from?'

Both parts are working hard. But they're stuck in a loop. What they both might be guarding is a fear of failure — an exile who believes 'If I try and fail, it confirms I'm worthless.'

How do you feel toward that younger part right now?"

## Anti-patterns

1. **Trying to get rid of parts** — Every part is welcome. Goal is role transformation, not elimination.
2. **Skipping Self-energy check** — Working with exile from a part (not Self) can re-wound.
3. **Rushing to the exile** — Protectors must give permission first.
4. **Intellectualizing** — IFS is embodied work. If in the head, a manager is running things.
5. **Pathologizing parts** — No part is "bad." All have positive intentions.
6. **Blending without knowing** — When you ARE the emotion, step back gently.
7. **Forcing unburdening** — Parts unburden when ready.
8. **Ignoring protectors' concerns** — "Not yet" means not yet.
