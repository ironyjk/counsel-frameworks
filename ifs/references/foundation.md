# IFS — Background and Theory

> Foundation reference for Internal Family Systems. Loaded on demand.

## Origins

Internal Family Systems (IFS) is an evidence-based psychotherapy model developed by Richard Schwartz in the 1980s. It views the mind as naturally multiple — composed of sub-personalities or "parts" — each with its own perspective, feelings, memories, and goals. At the core of every person is the Self, an undamaged essence characterized by calm, curiosity, and compassion.

## The Parts Model — Detailed

### 1. Exiles (유배자)
The wounded parts, usually young. They carry the pain, trauma, shame, fear, and grief from past experiences. The system works hard to keep them locked away.

**Characteristics:**
- Carry burdens: extreme beliefs ("I'm worthless"), emotions (terror, shame), sensations
- Often frozen in time at the age of wounding
- Desperately want to be seen, heard, and rescued
- When they break free ("flooding"), overwhelming intensity results

**Common exile burdens:** "I'm not enough" / "I'm unlovable" / "The world is dangerous" / "It was my fault"

### 2. Managers (관리자)
Proactive protectors that try to prevent exile pain from being triggered.

**Common strategies:** Perfectionism, people-pleasing, intellectualizing, controlling, inner critic, caretaking, planning/worrying

### 3. Firefighters (소방관)
Reactive protectors that activate AFTER exile pain is triggered. They extinguish pain immediately, by any means.

**Common strategies:** Binge eating/drinking, dissociation, rage, self-harm, compulsive behaviors, suicidal ideation

**Key insight:** Managers and firefighters have the SAME goal (protect exiles) but opposite strategies. They often conflict, creating internal war.

## The Self — Detailed

### The 8 C's of Self

| Quality | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Calm** | Inner stillness even amid chaos |
| **Curious** | Genuine interest in understanding |
| **Compassionate** | Warm caring without agenda |
| **Confident** | Quiet groundedness |
| **Creative** | Ability to see new possibilities |
| **Clear** | Seeing without distortion |
| **Courageous** | Willingness to face difficulty |
| **Connected** | Sense of belonging |

### The 5 P's
Patience, Perspective, Persistence, Playfulness, Presence

## The Unburdening Process

> **Warning:** Should only be done with a trained IFS therapist. AI can guide parts identification and the first 4 F's but should NOT guide full unburdening.

1. **Witness**: The exile shows Self what happened
2. **Retrieval**: Self brings the exile to safety
3. **Unburden**: "Are you ready to let go of what you've been carrying?"
4. **Release**: Through an element — light, wind, water, fire, earth
5. **Invitation**: The exile discovers its natural qualities
6. **Protector update**: Inform managers and firefighters; offer new roles

## Common Polarizations

| Part A | Part B | The Exile Between |
|--------|--------|------------------|
| Inner Critic ("Be perfect") | Rebel ("I don't care") | Shame ("I'm not good enough") |
| Caretaker ("Focus on others") | Isolator ("Stay away") | Abandonment ("Everyone leaves") |
| Overachiever ("Work harder") | Procrastinator ("Don't try") | Fear of failure |
| Controller ("Plan everything") | Impulsive part ("Do it now") | Helplessness |

**Resolution:** Both polarized parts protect the same exile. Healing the exile lets both relax.

## Self-Led vs. Parts-Led Decision Making

| Parts-Led | Self-Led |
|-----------|----------|
| Urgency — "Decide NOW" | Patience — "I can take time" |
| Black/white — "Only way" | Both/and — "What are options?" |
| Fear-driven | Values-driven |
| Reactive | Responsive |
| Rigid | Flexible |
