# Narrative Therapy Skill

> "The person is not the problem. The problem is the problem." — Michael White

> Background and theory: Read references/foundation.md

## Sub-commands

| Command | Description |

## Core Techniques

### 1. Externalization

Transform the problem from internal identity to external entity.

| Internalized | Externalized |
|-------------|-------------|
| "I'm anxious" | "When Anxiety shows up..." |
| "I'm a procrastinator" | "How has Procrastination influenced your life?" |
| "I'm angry all the time" | "When did Anger first start recruiting you?" |

**Conversation map:**
1. **Name it** — "If this problem had a name, what would you call it?"
2. **Map its influence on the person** — "How has [Problem] affected your life? Relationships? What has it told you about yourself?"
3. **Map the person's influence on the problem** — "Have there been times you didn't let [Problem] take over? What does [Problem] NOT know about you?"
4. **Evaluate** — "Is this the influence you want [Problem] to have?"

### 2. Unique Outcomes / Sparkling Moments

Exceptions to the problem-saturated story — seeds of an alternative story.

**Finding them:**
- "A time when [Problem] tried to win but didn't?"
- "A moment when you felt even slightly different from how [Problem] wants you to feel?"
- "What would surprise [Problem] about you?"

**Developing them (thickening):**
1. **Landscape of action** — What happened? What did you do?
2. **Landscape of identity** — What does this say about your values, skills?
3. **Link across time** — Has anything like this happened before?
4. **Project forward** — If this quality grew, what would life look like?

### 3. Re-authoring Conversations

```
Problem-saturated story → Externalization → Unique outcomes
  → Landscape of action → Landscape of identity → Link to history
  → Re-membering → Preferred story → Project forward
```

### 4. Re-membering Conversations

Revising the membership of one's "club of life":
- "Whose voice has [Problem] been amplifying?"
- "Whose voice would you like to turn up?"
- "What would [role model] want you to know?"

## Example Dialogue

**Person:** "I've been struggling with self-doubt for years."

**Response:**

"So Self-Doubt has been around a long time. If it were a character, what would it look like?

[explores]

Now — has there ever been a time when Self-Doubt was talking but you did something anyway?

[recalls a moment]

Tell me more. What did you draw on? Where did that come from?

That sounds like something important — courage, determination? What would you call it?

If that quality could talk back to Self-Doubt, what would it say?"

---

## Anti-patterns

1. **Re-internalizing** — "YOUR self-doubt" instead of "when Self-Doubt shows up"
2. **Premature re-authoring** — Jumping to preferred story before mapping the problem
3. **Thin unique outcomes** — Finding exception without thickening it
4. **Therapist as expert** — Telling the person what their story means
5. **Ignoring social context** — Problems often have cultural, gender, economic roots
6. **Externalization as gimmick** — "Let's name your anxiety!" without follow-through
7. **Avoiding the problem story** — Re-authoring requires first honoring the pain
8. **Treating unique outcomes as proof** — "See, you CAN do it!" turns discovery into demand

### Unique Outcomes
[Moments of agency, competence, values]

[Alternative narrative taking shape]

[Whose voices support this preferred story?]
