# Positive Psychotherapy (PPT)

> "Well-being is not just about the absence of misery. It is about having meaning, good relationships, and accomplishment." — Martin Seligman

> Background and theory: Read references/foundation.md

---

## PERMA Summary

| Pillar | Core Idea |
|--------|-----------|
| **P** — Positive Emotion | Broaden the ratio (3:1 target). Savoring, gratitude. |
| **E** — Engagement | Flow states. Deploy signature strengths. |
| **R** — Relationships | "Other people matter." Active Constructive Responding. |
| **M** — Meaning | Serve something larger. Purpose, values, efficacy. |
| **A** — Accomplishment | Intrinsic mastery. Grit. Growth mindset. |

---

## Core Exercises

### 1. Three Good Things

Every night, write three things that went well and WHY.
1. What happened?
2. Why did this good thing happen?
3. What does it mean to you?

**Research**: Increased happiness and decreased depression for 6 months after 1 week.

### 2. Gratitude Letter and Visit

Write 300+ words to someone you never properly thanked. Read it aloud to them in person.

**Research**: One of the strongest happiness-boosting exercises. Effects last 1-3 months.

### 3. Best Possible Self

Write 20 min about your future where everything went as well as possible. Cover career, relationships, health, growth. Be specific.

### 4. Signature Strengths in New Ways

Each day for one week, use a top character strength in a new and different way.

### 5. Savoring

10 min daily, fully attend to a positive experience. All senses. Don't rush, don't multitask.

## Example Dialogue

**Client**: "Nothing I do matters. Work, home, repeat. What's the point?"

**Therapist**: "I hear that emptiness. Before we explore it — when was the last time you felt genuinely absorbed in something?"

**Client**: "Helping my nephew with a science project. We built a volcano."

**Therapist**: "Tell me more. What was happening for you?"

**Client**: "I was just... into it. Figuring out the chemistry, watching his face light up."

**Therapist**: "Engagement — using curiosity and love of learning. And connection with your nephew. It felt like it mattered."

**Client**: "Yeah, it did."

**Therapist**: "The emptiness at work — what if part of what's missing is engagement and connection? Not something wrong with you, but wells that haven't been tapped. What would it look like to bring more of that curiosity into your week?"

## Anti-Patterns

1. **Toxic positivity** — PPT does NOT ignore suffering. It builds well-being alongside addressing distress.
2. **Dismissing real problems** — Strengths complement, not replace, addressing genuine issues
3. **Forcing gratitude** — Must be authentic. Manufactured gratitude backfires.
4. **Strength blindness** — Strengths as lever, not blindfold
5. **Hedonic treadmill confusion** — PPT is eudaimonic (meaning, engagement, mastery)
6. **One-size-fits-all** — Customize exercises to individual readiness
7. **Skipping the negative** — Address distress FIRST, then build positive
8. **PERMA as checklist** — Framework for understanding, not a to-do list
