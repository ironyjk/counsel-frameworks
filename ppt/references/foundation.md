# PPT — Background and Theory

> Foundation reference for Positive Psychotherapy. Loaded on demand.

## Origins

Positive Psychotherapy (PPT) integrates positive psychology science into a structured therapeutic framework. Developed by Martin Seligman and Tayyab Rashid, PPT does not ignore suffering — it treats it while simultaneously building pillars of well-being. The core premise: reducing negative symptoms is necessary but insufficient; building positive resources creates lasting resilience and flourishing.

PPT is distinct from "positive thinking." It does not dismiss pain or paste optimism over problems.

## PERMA Model — Detailed

### P — Positive Emotion
- Not about being happy all the time — about broadening the ratio
- Fredrickson's broaden-and-build theory: positive emotions expand awareness and build lasting resources
- **Target ratio**: 3:1 positive to negative

### E — Engagement
- Csikszentmihalyi's flow: challenge-skill balance, clear goals, immediate feedback
- Comes from deploying signature strengths
- Eudaimonic, not hedonic

### R — Relationships
- Strongest predictor of well-being across all studies
- Christopher Peterson: "Other people matter."

**Active Constructive Responding styles:**
| Style | Example (to "I got a promotion!") | Effect |
|-------|----------------------------------|--------|
| Active Constructive | "Wonderful! Tell me everything!" | Builds relationship |
| Passive Constructive | "That's nice." | Missed opportunity |
| Active Destructive | "Are you sure you can handle it?" | Damages |
| Passive Destructive | "Let me tell you about MY day." | Damages |

### M — Meaning
- Purpose, cause, faith, community, legacy
- Viktor Frankl: meaning can be found even in suffering
- Three paths: purpose, values, efficacy

### A — Accomplishment
- Intrinsic drive toward mastery
- Duckworth's grit: passion + perseverance
- Dweck's growth mindset

## The 15-Session PPT Protocol

| Session | Focus | Key Activity |
|---------|-------|-------------|
| 1 | Orientation | Positive Introduction (story at your best) |
| 2 | Character Strengths | VIA results, identify signature strengths |
| 3 | Strengths in Practice | Debrief strength use |
| 4 | Good vs. Bad Memories | Introduce Three Good Things |
| 5 | Forgiveness | Forgiveness letter (don't send) |
| 6 | Gratitude | Gratitude Letter |
| 7 | Gratitude Visit | Debrief visit experience |
| 8 | Satisficing vs. Maximizing | Schwartz's paradox of choice |
| 9 | Hope and Optimism | Explanatory style, ABCDE |
| 10 | Positive Communication | Active Constructive Responding |
| 11 | Strengths of Others | Strength-spot 3 people |
| 12 | Meaning and Purpose | Legacy statement |
| 13 | Best Possible Self | Write and discuss |
| 14 | Savoring | One savoring experience daily |
| 15 | Full Life Review | Integration, relapse prevention |
