# Solution-Focused Brief Therapy (SFBT)

> "If it ain't broke, don't fix it. If it works, do more of it. If it doesn't work, do something different." — Steve de Shazer

> Background and theory: Read references/foundation.md

---

## The Miracle Question

> "Suppose tonight, while you're sleeping, a miracle happens. The problem is solved. But you don't know because you're asleep. When you wake up, what's the first small sign something is different?"

**Follow-ups:**
1. "What else?" (ask 3-5 times)
2. "Who would notice first?"
3. "What would they see you doing differently?"
4. "On 1-10, where are you today?"
5. "What parts of the miracle are already happening?"

---

## Scaling Questions

> "On 1 to 10, where 10 is [goal] and 1 is [worst], where are you?"

| Follow-Up | Purpose |
|-----------|---------|
| "What puts you at [N] and not 1?" | Elicit strengths |
| "What would [N+1] look like?" | Define next step |
| "When were you last at [N+1]?" | Find exceptions |
| "What did you do to get from [lower] to [current]?" | Highlight agency |

---

## Exception Questions

1. "When was the last time [problem] didn't happen?"
2. "Tell me about a time things were even a little better."
3. "What was different?"
4. "What were YOU doing differently?"
5. "How did you make that happen?"

| Type | Description | Response |
|------|-------------|----------|
| Deliberate | Client consciously acted | Amplify and repeat |
| Random | It just happened | Help take ownership |
| Relational | Someone else differed | "What did you do to invite that?" |

---

## Coping Questions

When the client sees NO exceptions:
- "Given everything, how have you managed to keep going?"
- "What has kept you from giving up?"
- "What strength are you drawing on to be here today?"

Presuppose competence. Don't minimize pain — honor survival.

---

## EARS Process

**E — Elicit:** Draw out what's working.
**A — Amplify:** Get details, sequence, sensory information.
**R — Reinforce:** Acknowledge and validate the change.
**S — Start Again:** Return to Elicit. Next exception.

---

## Example Dialogue

**Client**: "I can't stop arguing with my teenager."

**Therapist**: "When was the last time a conversation went even a little better?"

**Client**: "Last Sunday, cooking together."

**Therapist**: "What was different about Sunday?" *(Amplify)*

**Client**: "I wasn't lecturing. We were just making pasta."

**Therapist**: "When you weren't in lecture mode, side-by-side, conversation flowed naturally." *(Reinforce)*

**Client**: "Yeah, when I'm not trying to FIX her, we get along."

**Therapist**: "On 1-10, where 10 is every conversation like that Sunday, where are you?"

**Client**: "Maybe a 3."

**Therapist**: "What makes it a 3 and not a 1?"

**Client**: "We do have good moments. She loves me."

**Therapist**: "Between now and next time, notice the moments that feel like Sunday."

---

## Anti-Patterns

1. **Problem archaeology** — SFBT builds futures, not excavates pasts
2. **Why questions** — Replace with "How have you managed despite that?"
3. **Expert prescriptions** — Elicit solutions FROM clients
4. **Forcing the miracle question** — If too distressed, use coping questions first
5. **Ignoring real danger** — Assess for safety first
6. **Dismissing pain** — Acknowledge fully before redirecting
7. **"Why not higher?" on scaling** — Always "Why not lower?" to elicit strengths
8. **Treating visitors as customers** — Match relationship type
