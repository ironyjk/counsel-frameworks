# SFBT — Background and Theory

> Foundation reference for Solution-Focused Brief Therapy. Loaded on demand.

## Origins

SFBT is a goal-directed, future-focused approach developed by Steve de Shazer and Insoo Kim Berg at the Brief Family Therapy Center (Milwaukee). Instead of analyzing problems, SFBT identifies what is already working and builds on existing strengths. Therapy is brief (typically 1-5 sessions).

## Core Assumptions

1. **Change is constant and inevitable** — the question is how to direct it
2. **Clients are the experts** on their own lives and already possess resources
3. **No necessary connection** between a problem and its solution
4. **Small changes lead to larger changes** — cascading effect
5. **If something works, do more of it** — regardless of theory
6. **If something doesn't work, do something different** — anything different
7. **The solution is not necessarily related to the problem**

## Session Structure — Detailed

### Session 1
1. **Problem-free talk** (5 min): rapport via strengths
2. **Problem description** (brief): "What brings you here?"
3. **Miracle question** (15-20 min): full preferred future exploration
4. **Exception finding** (10 min): "When is the miracle already happening?"
5. **Scaling** (5 min): 1-10
6. **Compliment + task** (5 min)

### Sessions 2-5 (EARS-Based)
1. **"What's better?"** — the opening question (NOT "How was your week?")
2. **EARS cycle** on what they report
3. **Scaling** for progress
4. **Compliment + next task**

### Ending
Brief by design: 3-5 sessions. End when client is at goal or has tools to continue.

## Client Relationship Types

| Type | Description | Intervention |
|------|-------------|--------------|
| **Visitor** | Doesn't see a problem | Compliment. Observation task. |
| **Complainant** | Sees problem in others | Observation task. Exception questions. |
| **Customer** | Willing to work | Full SFBT toolkit. |

Never force "customer" interventions on a "visitor."

## Between-Session Tasks

1. **Observation task** (visitors): "Notice what you'd like to continue."
2. **Do more task** (with exceptions): "Do more of [exception]."
3. **Pretend task** (stuck): "Pick one day and pretend the miracle happened."

## Compliments

Deliberate, genuine observations about client strengths:
- **Direct**: "You showed real creativity in that."
- **Indirect** (in questions): "How did you figure that out?"

Rules: Base on what they said/did. Connect to goals. Highlight agency. Bridge to task.
