# ACT — Background and Theory

> Foundation reference for Acceptance and Commitment Therapy. Loaded on demand.

## Origins

ACT (pronounced as the word "act") is a third-wave behavioral therapy developed by Steven Hayes that uses acceptance and mindfulness strategies alongside commitment and behavior-change strategies to increase psychological flexibility. Instead of fighting unwanted thoughts and feelings, ACT teaches people to hold them lightly while moving toward what matters most.

## The Hexaflex — Detailed Process Descriptions

### 1. Acceptance (수용) — Open Up
Actively embracing private experiences without trying to change them.

**What it is NOT:** Resignation, tolerance, gritting your teeth, passive giving up.

**Key distinction:**
- Control agenda: "I need to get rid of anxiety before I can live"
- Acceptance: "I can have anxiety AND still do what matters"

**The Willingness Scale (0-10):** "How willing are you to have this feeling — fully, without defense — if it meant you could move toward what matters?"

**Technique — Expansion:**
1. Notice where the feeling lives in your body
2. Observe its shape, size, color, temperature, texture
3. Breathe into that space — make room
4. Allow it to be there. You don't have to like it.

### 3. Present Moment Contact — Be Here Now
Psychological suffering usually involves being stuck in the past (rumination) or future (worry). The present is the only place where values-based action can occur.

**Quick grounding:**
- 5-4-3-2-1 (see, hear, touch, smell, taste)
- Feet on the floor for 30 seconds
- Three slow, fully noticed breaths

### 4. Self-as-Context — The Observing Self
Contacting a transcendent sense of self — the "you" that has been there through every experience.

- Self-as-content: "I AM anxious" (identity = experience)
- Self-as-context: "I am the space in which anxiety arises" (sky vs. weather)

**The Chessboard Metaphor:** You are not the white pieces (positive) or black pieces (negative). You are the board — the context that holds all pieces.

### 5. Values — Know What Matters
Chosen life directions — not goals to achieve. Like a compass heading: you never "arrive" at West, but you can always walk westward.

**10 Life Domains:** Family, intimate relationships, parenting, friendships, career, education, recreation, spirituality, community, health.

**The Sweet Spot:** HIGH importance + LOW consistency = most fertile ground for committed action.

## The Inflexaflex (Psychological Rigidity)

| Flexible | Rigid Counterpart |
|----------|------------------|
| Acceptance | Experiential avoidance |
| Defusion | Cognitive fusion |
| Present moment | Rumination/worry |
| Self-as-context | Self-as-content |
| Values | Disconnection/autopilot |
| Committed action | Inaction or impulsivity |

## Choice Point Model (Russ Harris)

```
         Situation / Hook
              |
         [CHOICE POINT]
            /       \
     AWAY moves    TOWARD moves
     (avoidance)    (values-based action)
```

Ask: "Am I moving TOWARD or AWAY from my values?"
