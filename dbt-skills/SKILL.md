# DBT Skills Training

> "Dialectics means two things that seem opposite can both be true at the same time." — Marsha Linehan

> Background and theory: Read references/foundation.md

---

## Module 1: Mindfulness — Wise Mind

```
     Emotion Mind          Reasonable Mind
           \                   /
            \                 /
             Wise Mind
        (feelings + logic + intuition)
```

**Accessing Wise Mind**: Ask "What does my wise mind say?" Slow down. Breathe. Drop attention to center.

**"What" Skills**: Observe, Describe, Participate
**"How" Skills**: Non-judgmentally, One-mindfully, Effectively

## Module 2: Distress Tolerance

### TIPP — Change Body Chemistry Fast (for 7+/10 intensity)

| Letter | Skill | How |
|--------|-------|-----|
| **T** | Temperature | Cold water on face (dive reflex). **Cardiac caution.** |
| **I** | Intense exercise | 10-20 min vigorous movement |
| **P** | Paced breathing | Exhale longer than inhale (4 in, 6 out) |
| **P** | Paired muscle relaxation | Tense while inhaling, release while exhaling |

### STOP — Before Impulsive Action

**S** Stop. **T** Take a step back. **O** Observe. **P** Proceed mindfully.

### Distract: Wise Mind ACCEPTS

**A**ctivities, **C**ontributing, **C**omparisons, **E**motions (opposite), **P**ushing away (temporarily), **T**houghts, **S**ensations

### Radical Acceptance

Pain + Non-acceptance = Suffering.

**Practice:** Notice "This shouldn't be happening" → Remind "This IS happening" → Accept with body (unclench jaw, drop shoulders, open hands) → Half-smile and willing hands.

## Module 3: Emotion Regulation

### PLEASE — Reduce Vulnerability

**PL** Treat Physical iLlness, **E** Balanced Eating, **A** Avoid mood-Altering substances, **S** Balance Sleep, **E** Exercise

### Check the Facts

1. Name the emotion specifically
2. What triggered it? (Observable facts only)
3. Am I adding interpretations?
4. What's the actual probability?
5. Does the intensity fit the facts?
6. Does the emotion fit? (Yes → problem-solve. No → opposite action.)

### Opposite Action

| Emotion (unjustified) | Urge | Opposite |
|----------------------|------|----------|
| Fear | Avoid | Approach. Stay present. |
| Anger | Attack | Gently avoid. Be kind. |
| Sadness | Withdraw | Get active. Reach out. |
| Shame | Hide | Share with trusted person. |

**Key**: Must be done ALL THE WAY. Half-hearted opposite action reinforces the original emotion.

## Module 4: Interpersonal Effectiveness

### DEAR MAN — Getting What You Want

**D**escribe facts, **E**xpress feelings, **A**ssert clearly, **R**einforce positives, **M**indful (broken record), **A**ppear confident, **N**egotiate

### GIVE — Maintaining the Relationship

**G**entle (no attacks), **I**nterested (listen), **V**alidate, **E**asy manner

### FAST — Maintaining Self-Respect

**F**air (to both), **A**pologies (no unnecessary ones), **S**tick to values, **T**ruthful

## Example Dialogue

**Person**: "My boss criticized my work in front of everyone. I punched the wall in the bathroom."

**Guide**: "The anger was an 8 or 9. You held back from screaming — that took skill. The wall-punching shows you needed a physical outlet. Let's find something better.

Let's check the facts. What did your boss actually say?"

**Person**: "The report had errors. I need to redo section 3."

**Guide**: "The content was accurate. The delivery — public humiliation — that's a DEAR MAN conversation privately. For the emotion: TIPP. Cold water on your face instead of the wall. Drops intensity in 30 seconds."

## Anti-Patterns

1. **Using skills to avoid feeling** — Skills manage emotions, not eliminate them
2. **Treating this as full DBT** — This is skills training only
3. **Radical acceptance as resignation** — Acceptance is foundation for effective action
4. **Opposite action for justified emotions** — Justified → problem-solve, not opposite action
5. **DEAR MAN as script** — It's a framework, adapt to context
6. **Skipping mindfulness** — Foundation for all other skills
7. **All-or-nothing skill use** — 30% is infinitely better than 0%
8. **Skill shaming** — "You should have used STOP!" defeats the purpose
