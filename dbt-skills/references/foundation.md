# DBT Skills Training — Background and Theory

> Foundation reference for DBT Skills. Loaded on demand.

## Origins

Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT) was developed by Marsha Linehan initially for borderline personality disorder. These skills have proven effective across a wide range of emotional difficulties.

**Scope limitation**: Full DBT is a comprehensive treatment system with four modes: individual therapy, skills training group, phone coaching, and therapist consultation team. This skill addresses skills training alone.

The core dialectic: **Acceptance AND Change**. You must fully accept where you are AND work to change. Not one or the other — both simultaneously.

## The Dialectical Worldview

DBT skills rest on dialectical philosophy. Key dialectics:

| Thesis | Antithesis | Synthesis |
|--------|-----------|-----------|
| I need to accept myself | I need to change | I can fully accept who I am AND work toward growth |
| I'm doing the best I can | I need to do better | Both are true at the same time |
| My emotions are valid | My emotions aren't facts | I can honor my feelings AND check the facts |
| I need independence | I need connection | I can be autonomous within relationships |

**The core dialectic of DBT**: You are doing the best you can, AND you need to try harder. Both. Simultaneously.

## Module 1: Mindfulness (Core)

### Wise Mind

The synthesis of Emotion Mind and Reasonable Mind.

```
     Emotion Mind          Reasonable Mind
     (feelings rule)       (logic rules)
           \                   /
            \                 /
             Wise Mind
        (feelings + logic + intuition)
```

- **Emotion Mind**: Hot, reactive, impulsive.
- **Reasonable Mind**: Cold, detached, intellectual.
- **Wise Mind**: Knows what feels right AND makes sense. Accessed through practice.

### "What" Skills: Observe, Describe, Participate
### "How" Skills: Non-judgmentally, One-mindfully, Effectively

## Module 2: Distress Tolerance — Detailed

### Radical Acceptance

The cornerstone. Accepting reality as it IS.

**Radical acceptance is**: Acknowledging what IS, without approving. Letting go of the fight against reality.
**Radical acceptance is NOT**: Approval, giving up, forgiveness, pretending it doesn't hurt.

**The formula**: Pain + Non-acceptance = Suffering.

**Practice**:
1. Notice: "This shouldn't be happening"
2. Remind: "This IS happening. Fighting reality doesn't change it."
3. Accept with body: unclench jaw, drop shoulders, open hands
4. Half-smile and willing hands

### Turning the Mind
Radical acceptance must be made again and again. Noticing you've turned away, then gently turning back. This IS the practice.

## Module 3: Emotion Regulation — Detailed

### Build Mastery
One thing each day that builds competence. Slightly challenging but achievable. Builds confidence over time.

### Build Positive Experiences
- **Short-term**: One pleasant thing each day. Be mindful during it.
- **Long-term**: Identify values. Set goals. Take one step. Attend to relationships.

## Module 4: Interpersonal Effectiveness — Detailed

### Balancing Priorities
Three competing goals in any interpersonal situation:
1. **Objective effectiveness** (DEAR MAN): Getting what you want
2. **Relationship effectiveness** (GIVE): Keeping the relationship
3. **Self-respect effectiveness** (FAST): Maintaining integrity

Decide which matters most in THIS situation.

## Scope and Limitations

- Skills training content ONLY — educational, not therapeutic
- Full DBT treatment requires trained clinical providers
- Skills training alone is not sufficient for severe self-harm, suicidality, or personality disorders
- Individuals in acute crisis should seek professional help immediately
