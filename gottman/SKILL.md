# Gottman Method

> "Happy marriages are based on a deep friendship." — Dr. John Gottman

> Background and theory: Read references/foundation.md

**Disclaimer**: Educational pattern-learning only, not couples therapy.

---

## The Sound Relationship House (Summary)

| Level | Name | Key |
|-------|------|-----|
| 1 | Love Maps | Know your partner's inner world |
| 2 | Fondness & Admiration | Express appreciation daily |
| 3 | Turn Toward | Respond to bids 86%+ of the time |
| 4 | Positive Perspective | Benefit of the doubt (requires L1-3) |
| 5 | Manage Conflict | 69% perpetual — dialogue, not resolution |
| 6 | Life Dreams | Support each other's aspirations |
| 7 | Shared Meaning | Rituals, goals, values, narrative |
| Walls | Trust & Commitment | Load-bearing — hold everything up |

## The Four Horsemen and Antidotes

### 1. Criticism (비난)
**What:** Attacking character, not behavior. "You NEVER..." / "What's wrong with you?"
**Antidote: Gentle Startup** — "I feel [emotion] about [specific situation]. I need [request]."

### 2. Contempt (경멸)
**What:** Moral superiority. Sarcasm, eye-rolling, sneering, name-calling. **Single best predictor of divorce.**
**Antidote: Culture of Appreciation** — Express appreciation daily. Fondness and admiration.

### 3. Defensiveness (방어)
**What:** Counter-attack, righteous indignation, victim-playing.
**Antidote: Accept Responsibility** — Even for a small part. "You're right, I could have handled that better."

### 4. Stonewalling (담쌓기)
**What:** Withdrawing entirely. Silent treatment. Usually = **physiological flooding** (HR 100+ BPM).
**Antidote: Self-Soothing** — 20-minute break. "I need a break. I'll come back." Then actually return.

## Key Techniques

### Softened Startup
How it STARTS predicts how it ENDS (96%). Start with "I" not "You." Describe situation, not character. State need.

### Repair Attempts
- "Can we start over?"
- "I'm sorry. I got defensive."
- Reaching for partner's hand
- "I love you, even when we fight."

**Only effective when Levels 1-3 are funded.**

### The 5:1 Ratio
5 positive interactions per 1 negative during conflict = stable relationship.

## Example Dialogue

**Partner A**: "You forgot groceries AGAIN. I can't rely on you for anything."

**Analysis**: Criticism + contempt.

**Softened:** "I noticed groceries didn't get picked up. I'm frustrated because I was counting on it. Can we figure out a system?"

**Partner B (accepting responsibility)**: "You're right, I forgot. Sorry. What if I set a phone reminder?"

---

## Anti-Patterns

1. **Treating as couples therapy** — This is educational
2. **Weaponizing horsemen** — "You're being contemptuous!" is itself criticism
3. **Forcing the 20-minute break** — Must be genuine self-soothing
4. **Using 5:1 as scorecard** — Spirit over math
5. **Ignoring power dynamics** — Abuse requires different frameworks
6. **Diagnosing your partner** — Notice patterns, don't assign pathology
7. **Skipping Levels 1-3** — Conflict management without friendship = sand
