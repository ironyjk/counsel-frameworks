# Gottman Method — Background and Theory

> Foundation reference for Gottman Method. Loaded on demand.

**Nominative use disclaimer**: This describes research findings as published by Drs. John and Julie Gottman. Educational purposes only.

## Origins

The Gottman Method is grounded in over 40 years of research at the "Love Lab" (University of Washington). Their research identified specific, observable behaviors that predict relationship success or failure with over 90% accuracy.

**Core finding**: The difference between happy and unhappy couples is not the absence of conflict, but HOW they handle conflict. All couples fight. Masters repair quickly. Disasters let negativity escalate.

## The Sound Relationship House — Detailed

### Level 1: Build Love Maps
**Love Maps** = cognitive room for knowing your partner's inner world. Current worries, dreams, important people, favorites, life history, what's happening THIS week.

### Level 2: Share Fondness and Admiration
Express appreciation daily. Verbalize admiration. Tell others positive things about your partner. Recall positive history.

### Level 3: Turn Toward Instead of Away
**Bids** = fundamental unit of emotional connection. Any attempt to connect.

**Three responses**: Turning toward (builds trust), turning away (erodes slowly), turning against (actively damages).

**Research**: Masters turn toward 86% of the time. Disasters only 33%.

### Level 4: The Positive Perspective
Levels 1-3 strong → Positive Sentiment Override (benefit of the doubt).
Levels 1-3 weak → Negative Sentiment Override (even positive acts interpreted negatively).

### Level 5: Manage Conflict
69% of conflicts are **perpetual problems** — they never fully resolve. The goal: move from gridlock to dialogue.

### Level 6: Make Life Dreams Come True
Support each other's individual aspirations and purpose.

### Level 7: Create Shared Meaning
Rituals of connection, shared goals, shared values, shared narratives.

## Key Techniques — Detailed

### Dreams Within Conflict
For perpetual, gridlocked problems: explore the deeper dream inside each position.

**Process:**
1. Each partner describes their position
2. "What does this mean to you?" "Is there a story behind this?"
3. Understand the dream inside the position
4. Find flexibility: "What's non-negotiable? What's flexible?"
5. Create temporary compromise honoring both dreams

### Repair Attempts
Any statement or action preventing negativity from escalating. The secret weapon.

**Examples:** "Can we start over?" / "I'm sorry, I got defensive." / Reaching for partner's hand / Using humor

**Key insight:** Repair attempts only work when the emotional bank account (Levels 1-3) is sufficiently funded.

### The 5:1 Ratio
Stable relationships: at least 5 positive interactions for every 1 negative during conflict.
- 5:1+: Stable, satisfying
- Below 5:1: At risk
- 0.8:1: Predicts divorce

## Scope and Limitations

- Pattern learning only, not couples therapy
- Not appropriate for relationships involving abuse, addiction, or active infidelity without professional support
- Cultural context matters: research primarily on North American couples
- Individual therapy needs should be addressed alongside or before couples work
